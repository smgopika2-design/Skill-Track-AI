Skill Decay

Why skills fade — and why it matters

Skill retention isn't permanent. Without reinforcement, memory traces weaken, leading to knowledge gaps that silently erode performance.

📉 The Problem

Studies show humans forget up to 70% of new information within 24 hours without reinforcement. In technical education, this compounds rapidly across interconnected concepts.

🔁 Spaced Repetition Gap

Most learners rely on passive re-reading. Without active recall at spaced intervals, synaptic connections degrade and skill automaticity is lost.

📊 Impact on Careers

Technical skills that aren't practiced atrophy within 3–6 months. SkillTrack AI identifies this risk early — before it becomes visible in work output.

✅ The Solution

By modeling decay mathematically and training a Random Forest classifier on real student data, we can predict retention scores and prescribe targeted interventions.
